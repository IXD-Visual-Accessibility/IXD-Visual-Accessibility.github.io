ABCD Check

Here are a few ABCD's to remember while designing a website. This is a mnemonic we created so that you never forget.

  1. A - Alt Text. Always remeber Alt text in html
  2. B -Be Structured. Always structure your html for easy screen reading
  3. C - Check colour. Check colour pallette, contrast and centered content
  4. D - Design Consistent. Be mindful of visual and congitive disabilities and design consistent
  5. E - Enlarge Content. Large text is always easy to read.
  6. F - Follow WCAG. Follow these guidelines.

SLIDE Check

SLIDE Check is another checklist that one can follow to check parameters for accesibility friendliness.

  1. S - Style. Heading styles and descriptive titles
  2. L -Link. Use embeded hyperlinks
  3. I - Images. Use Alt text to describe images
  4. D - Design. Clear simple and consistent design
  5. E - Evaluation. Use accesibility checkers

Selwyn Goldsmith was an arhitect and a disability advocate. His books have become sources of inspiration and handbooks for architects and designers. He is famously known for innovating and introducing the idea of the dropped kerb. He worked on and presented multiple researches that highlighted problems with design of amenities we use as a civic society and is also known for introducing the concept of Universal Design.

Ronald Mace was a product designer and architect. He contracted Polio at a young age and used a wheelchair for the rest of his life. He spent a lot of time on advocacy for accessibility in building design. He was instrumental in the adoption of Chapter11X which was the first accessibility focussed building code adopted in the United States. This code became the north star model for other states to adopt. He was instrumental in the passing of many other disability related amendments and acts like the Rehabilitation Act of 1973. He also ran a consulting firm which focussed on accessibility and Universal Design.

Patricia Moore also known as the mother of Universal Design is an industrial designer and thought leader. She has designed products like OXO Smart-Grip potato peelers and is involved in designing for the ageing population. The geriatric population isn't traditionally considered under the bracket of disability and design for them still remains a weak area of focus and she is changing this. She has also published a lot of literature with regard to Universal Design.
