About the Project

Technological devices today have become an extension of our individual lives. We use our phones, laptops and other smart devices to complete a variety of everyday tasks. It can be natural to assume that people with disabilities also use technology extensively and in ways similar to regular users. However, this implies that the web experiences designed to be used are accessible to everyone and unfortunately this is not true. People with diverse abilities and contexts often find themselves excluded from digital products because designers overlook their needs and goals.
This project focuses on visually impaired people. Besides blindness, common types of visual impairment can include color blindness and low vision. Low vision can include partial sight in one or both eyes, poor acuity (blurry vision), tunnel vision, central field loss, and clouded vision. People with blurred vision face issues with sharpness and clarity. Tunnel vision is a vision defect where objects cannot be seen unless they are near the center of the visual field (“Tunnel Vision”, n.d). Therefore, we as UX designers need to think of UX solutions to address these problems to include the group of visually impaired.We hope that this project serves as a good starting point for designers in their journey towards building visually accessible products.
