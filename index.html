About Accessibility

Technological devices today have become an extension of our individual lives.People with disabilities also use technology extensively and in ways similar to other users,but unfortunately the experience is not the same for everyone. People with diverse abilities and contexts often find themselves excluded from digital products because designers overlook their needs and goals. Explore this section further to understand what accessibility entails. Further we dive deeper into visual accessibility.

Accessibility Resources

Accessibility means that the product and its features can be used by a wide range of users with different needs. It involves being able to adhere to the standard guidelines set by different institutions. A group of architects, designers and rehabilitation engineers developed a set of guidelines to lay the foundation for Universal Design. This section takes you through principles of accessible design and shares sources that can be used to help aid your design in the direction of accessibility.

Accessibility Museum

The history of Accessibility in America saw it's first milestone of recognition in 1961 with the introduction of the American National Standards Institute (ANSI) A117.1 for Accessible and Usable Buildings and Facilities. This standard was the first nationally recognised accessible design standard. Every innovation from the design of the typewriter to a game of Mahjong has accesibility at the centre of it. This section covers historic milestones and interesting facts about design and Accessibility.
