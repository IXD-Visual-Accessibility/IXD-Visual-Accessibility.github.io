What is accessibility and why does it matter?

What is Accessibility

Accessibility means how easily and effectively a product or service can be accessed and used. The product and its features can be used by a wide range of users with different needs. When most people think about accessibility, they think about designing for people with disabilities. But that is only partially true. Sarah Horton defines disability in an equation:
Ability + Barrier= Disability
According to her, a person's functional capacity and the environment we have created are at odds. When websites and applications are poorly designed, barriers are put in place that prevent users from using the product. As a result, in the social view of disability, it is the product rather than the individual who forms the barrier.
In the efforts to improve the accessbility of products across different domains, a group of architects, designers and rehabilitation engineers developed a set of guidelines to lay the foundation for Universal Design under the guidance of Ron Mace at North Carolina State University. It outlines 7 basic principles to follow when designing accessible products:

  1. Equitable Use
    The design is useful and marketable to people with diverse abilities.
  2. Flexibility in Use
    The design accommodates a wide range of individual preferences and abilities.
  3. Simple and Intuitive Use
    Use of the design is easy to understand, regardless of the user's experience, knowledge, language skills, or current concentration level.
  4. Perceptible Information
    The design communicates necessary information effectively to the user, regardless of ambient conditions or the user's sensory abilities.
  5. Tolerance for Error
    The design minimizes hazards and the adverse consequences of accidental or unintended actions.
  6. Low Physical Effort
    The design can be used efficiently and comfortably and with a minimum of fatigue.
  7. Size and Space for Approach and Use
    Appropriate size and space is provided for approach, reach, manipulation, and use regardless of the user's body size, posture, or mobility.

You can find more details on Universal Design's official website.
