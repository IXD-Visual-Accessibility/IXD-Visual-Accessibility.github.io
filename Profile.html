Notable People

Meet the Pioneers

Jenny Lay-Flurrie

Jenny is the Chief Accessibility officer at Microsoft. She leads efforts at Microsoft to drive change through their website, products and services. She founded the Disability employee resource group at Microsoft and hosts the Microsoft Ability Summit that sees 2000+ attendees every year. She was also Instrumental in driving projects like "The Autism hiring program" and Soundscape. She has been recognised as the disability employment champion by the Whitehouse in October 2014.

Kat Holmes

Kat Holmes is known for her book "Mismatch:How Inclusion shapes Design". She has been the Principal director of Inclusive design at Microsoft from 2014-17.She spearheaded the making of Microsoft's Inclusive Design Toolkit that was inducted into Smithsonian's Cooper-Hewitt National Design Museum. Her core values of working are: There is no such thing as an average human, People should be in the lead and not just the centre of Technology and Work with people you enjoy being around, and have fun make something worth making.

Microsoft

Microsoft is one of the organisations doing pioneering work in the field of accesibile tech. They host the accessibility summit and have dedicated resources to enable accessibility. Some of their remarkable and notable products include the Xbox adaptive controller, Seeing AI, Microsoft Soundscape . They have also be instrumental in accesibility education and designing for accesibility is at the centre of their innovation. Microsoft has a dedicated department to ensure that inclusivity and Universal design remains at the center of their focus and is engrained in their culture.

Selwyn Goldsmith

Selwyn Goldsmith was an arhitect and a disability advocate. His books have become sources of inspiration and handbooks for architects and designers. He is famously known for innovating and introducing the idea of the dropped kerb. He worked on and presented multiple researches that highlighted problems with design of amenities we use as a civic society and is also known for introducing the concept of Universal Design.

Ronald Mace

Ronald Mace was a product designer and architect. He contracted Polio at a young age and used a wheelchair for the rest of his life. He spent a lot of time on advocacy for accessibility in building design. He was instrumental in the adoption of Chapter11X which was the first accessibility focussed building code adopted in the United States. This code became the north star model for other states to adopt. He was instrumental in the passing of many other disability related amendments and acts like the Rehabilitation Act of 1973. He also ran a consulting firm which focussed on accessibility and Universal Design.

Patricia Moore

Patricia Moore also known as the mother of Universal Design is an industrial designer and thought leader. She has designed products like OXO Smart-Grip potato peelers and is involved in designing for the ageing population. The geriatric population isn't traditionally considered under the bracket of disability and design for them still remains a weak area of focus and she is changing this. She has also published a lot of literature with regard to Universal Design.
