Timeline

A walk through history

Typewriter - 1800's

The typewriter is an invention born out of love and a story of friendship. Italian inventor Pellegrino Turri invented it so that his friend, the blind Countess Carolina Fantoni da Fivizzano could write letters.

Mahjong - 1800's

Mahjong originated in China in the 19th century and has been a game that can be played by the visually challenged as well.The tiles of Mahjong are textured differently hence they enable all players to play the game

Bendy Straw - 1930

The bendy straw was created by Joseph Friedman for his daughter. He saw that she couldn't drink using a straight straw and used dental floss and a screw to make a bendy straw. It was first embraced by hospitals to serve bed-ridden patients with food

Audiobooks - 1932

The idea of audiobooks was the brainchild of the 'American Foundation of the Blind'. The group recorded books on Vinyl records but as technology progressed we saw the coming of things like the Amazon audible audio player. Now audiobooks are used by everyone to listen and reduce screen-time.

Curb cuts - 1945

Selwyn Goldsmith and a few other architects who were disability advocates were the ones responsible for introducing curb cuts. Curb cuts were introduced for wheelchair accessibility but in the long run have gone to help people with strollers, suitcases or temporary disabilities like a fracture or injury.

Electric Toothbrush - 1954

The electric toothbrush by the name Broxodent electric toothbrush was invented in 1954 for people with limited mobility or strength. It was later widely adopted by all kinds of users and is now recommended for good dental health since it helps brush with the right technique.

Oxo Goodgrip - 1990

Sam Farber came up with the OXO grip. The invention was inspired from seeing his wife struggle with arthritis. He saw that his wife had difficulty gripping peelers and this led to him coming up with the grips for kitchen tools.

Speech-to-text - 1990

This was created for users who can't write. Initially it was created with limited functionality of being able to write, save and open documents with voice commands. Later versions were used in courtrooms and by medical transcriptionists but now we see much more evolved versions of this technology like voice assistants.

Fidget Spinners - 2017

Cathy Hetinger originally invented the gadget in 1993 to entertain her daughter, but the patent lapsed. It later became quite the rage in 2017 and was used by people with stress and ADHD. Ironically they were also banned from a few schools for being a distraction.
